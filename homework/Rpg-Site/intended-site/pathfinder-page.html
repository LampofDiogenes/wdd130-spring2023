Summary:

Pathfinder is an offshoot of DND 3.5, but has been far more embraced by the community. Pathfinder shares a lot of similarities with DND 5e with its fantasy adventure, but boasts nearly double the number of classes, with a staggering amount of prestige classes (which can be understodd as classes with specific requirements). on top of that, the customizability offered with feats is breathtaking.

Fighter

A fighter is a martial class. This class has the most feats out of any other class, and boasts the greatest degree of customizability. Fighters take on a very specific niche in their party, but the niche is very much dependent on your specialization and your feat path.

Cleric

Cleric is a spellcasting class, excelling in healing and support. It's primary ability is in wisdom, and casts divine spells. Each cleric choses a domain, which is usually dependent on the god that you worship.

Rogue

Rogue is a maritial class, excelling in skills and damage through sneak attack. They make excellent skill monkey, but may struggle to deal sneak attack damage. This is distinct from the 5e rogue, which is not as specific for using sneak attack

Sorcerer

Sorcerer is a versitile spellcasting class with a small spell list, but a spontaneous caster, meaning that they can cast any spell they know at any time (given they have an open spell slot). Sorcerers are Charism Casters
